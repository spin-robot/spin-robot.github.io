Comparison with state-of-the-art obstacle avoidance techniques

As per the suggestion of the reviewer, we compare our method with the most recent obstacle avoidance method for static as well as dynamic obstacles. We compare with the paper - "Vision-aided UAV navigation and dynamic obstacle avoidance using gradient-based B-spline trajectory optimization". Due to limitation of hardware, we test their approach in the simulator provided. While the reported accuracy for the approach is ~92% in simulation, the testing environments have significant distance between obstacles. For fair comparison, we test on same environment layouts as ours -- 4-5 obstacles within 5-6m range for 30 different rollouts. The baseline attains an accuracy of 77% for static obstacles and 60% for dynamic obstacles. Below we show some failure cases for both static (left) and dynamic (right) obstacle environments. The first frame in each video shows the goal position, subsequently show by the black cursor. As illustrated, most failure cases involve insufficient exploration, or incorrect planning when too close to the obstacles leading to locally suboptimal behavior, which makes recovery from failure harder. The baseline success rate obtained in simulation is similar or marginally lower to our performance in real-world and significantly lower to our simulation performance.
